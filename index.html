first week!

At a larger company, may have whole induction process, go away and get indoctrinated.

Small company, on your own, even medium size helps to be proactive.

  • access
  • product
  • process

Three things to go through

employee.grant_access(all_the_things)

Some may be handed to you, others you will have to ask, others you'll need to find out if they exist at all.

  • slack?
  • github
  • application
  • ci/cd
  • monitoring
  • ssh?
  • jira
  • hr/holidays?

Whichever of these apply. Named products for example purposes only.

Some will be a case of finding who has the access you may (occasionally) need, rather than getting your own credentials.

product.explore()

Some may be handed to you, others you will have to ask, others you'll need to find out if they exist at all.

  • use as a customer would
  • build something?
  • give (constructive!) feedback

As a fresh set of eyes, your early impressions should be really valuable to your new colleagues.

Applies, even if completely enterprisey product.

fix(bugs.find_one())

Some may be handed to you, others you will have to ask, others you'll need to find out if they exist at all.

  • find the bug tracker and how to use it
  • build/develop/run to reproduce the issue
  • fix it?
  • propose it
  • navigate review process
  • get deployed?

Get as far down this list as you can. Ideally can deploy within the week, but depending on current processes that may be a stretch.

summary

Take responsibility for making your first week a success.

Document whatever gaps you find, to help the next new starter.
